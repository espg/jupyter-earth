JupyterHub¶

JupyterHub is a multi-user Jupyter that can be deployed on the cloud or your own hardware. You may have used JupyterHub if you have ever logged in to Jupyter at an HPC center or on your campus.

The Littlest JupyterHub (TLJH)¶

TLJH is a simple JupyterHub distribution for a small (0-100) number of users on a single server, for example for a class or a small research group. In the docs, there us a guide: When to use The Littlest JupyterHub to determine if this is the right tool for you. The installation instructions include guides for deploying TLJH on: Digital Ocean, OVH, Jetstream, Google Cloud, Amazon Web Services, Azure, or your own server.

Zero to JupyterHub for Kubernetes (Z2JH)¶

The Z2JH guide outlines how to deploy JupyterHub on the commercial cloud. It is:

  • Scalable in both users and in resources

  • Uses Docker for environment management

  • Agnostic to the provider and hardware configuration
